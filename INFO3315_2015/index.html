INFO3315 Human Computer Interaction - Semester 2, 2015

Welcome to this first subject in Human Computer Interaction (HCI). It has three strands.

  • Core: essential skills for software engineerings to make use of established deisgn guidelines and usability techniques for conventional interface (eg web-based applications) and the many emerging ways for people to interact with technology.
  • Core: technical strand that builds skills to create web-based prototypes.
  • Advanced: hot-topics, new and emerging ways to interact with technology.
Assumed knowledge is a background in programming and operating systems sufficient to independently learn new programming tools from standard online technical materials, ability to conduct a literature search and to write reports based on guidelines for technical writing.
