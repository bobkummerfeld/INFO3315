edit Assessment
The final exam is worth 60% of the course mark. The remaining 40% is earned through participation and assignments as described in the unit outline.

Laboratory Participation - 5%
Assignment 1 - 10%, individual assignment due in week 7.
Assignment 2 - 10%, individual assignment due in week 10.
Assignment 3 - 15%, group report and presentation in week 11,12 or 13).
Examination - 60%

Please note: It is a policy of the School of Information Technologies that in order to pass this unit, a student must achieve at least 24/60 (40%) in the final examination and at least 16/40 (40%) in the combined mark for the assignments and class participation. A student must also achieve an overall final mark equal or greater than 50/100. Any student not meeting these requirements can achieve a maximum mark of no more than 45.

You should also read the policy and advice regarding academic honesty and performance on assessable items.

In fairness to all students late work cannot be accepted. In exceptional cases late work must be submitted directly to a course coordinator after a lecture, accompanied by an application for Special Consideration following your faculty system.

  Assignment 1 (due end of week 7)

This assignment is worth 10 marks, and it is due at 23:59pm on Friday September 12. The assignment should be handed in through eLearning (submitting a pdf file containing your answers). Each assignment must be individual work, and you will need to make an academic honesty declaration. You must include this signed document for your submission to be graded: http://sydney.edu.au/engineering/it/current_students/undergrad/policies/assignment_sheet_individual.pdf

The assessment will be based on aspects such as correct use of terminology, answers that show evidence of understanding of the concepts covered in the unit, originality and sophistication of thought, answers that specifically address the question asked, and clarity of presentation. We do not impose any particular length limit on your answers; however, we expect that a good answer to each question can be provided in about half-a-page. If you write more than this for any question, you should be sure that you have enough different and worthwhile ideas to justify the length (or else you will lose marks because of lack of clarity in presentation).

Question 1 [2 marks] This question concerns the main security goals (confidentiality, integrity, availability). Describe a situation in which the the goal of integrity is important, but there is not a need for confidentiality.

Question 2 [2 marks] In a particular form of two-factor authentication, a user shows their identity by providing a password and then uses an app on their mobile phone to generate a security code that must be entered as well. Describe an attack that would succeed despite this particular two-factor approach.

Question 3 [2 marks] It is not uncommon to receive email purporting to be from your bank stating that the online banking system has a problem and needs to be reset. In order to do this you are requested to send your login name and password to a certain address. Write an explanation suitable for a friend who is not studying any computing courses, to help explain to them about the dangers of sending the requested information.

Question 4 [2 marks] Some systems have a policy that every user must change their password at the beginning of every month. Discuss the impact of this policy on the normal work of the organization. Does the policy enhance security? What are the advantages and disadvantages of this policy?

Question 5 [2 marks] A typical password is about 8 characters long (and so can be stored in 8 bytes, or 64 bits). However, a typical key for encryption/decryption is much longer, and a key of 64 bits would not be considered secure. Explain this in terms of the difference between the way a secret is used as a password, and the way it is used as a key. Hint: what happens if an attacker takes a few months to discover the secret?

  Assignment 2 (due end of week 10)

This assignment is worth 10 marks, and it is due at 23:59pm on Friday 10th October. The assignment should be handed in through eLearning (submitting a pdf file containing your answers). Each assignment must be individual work, and you will need to make an academic honesty declaration. You must include this signed document for your submission to be graded: http://sydney.edu.au/engineering/it/current_students/undergrad/policies/assignment_sheet_individual.pdf

The assessment will be based on aspects such as correct use of terminology, answers that show evidence of understanding of the concepts covered in the unit, originality and sophistication of thought, answers that specifically address the question asked, and clarity of presentation. We do not impose any particular length limit on your answers; however, we expect that a good answer can be provided in two pages. If you write more than this for any question, you should be sure that you have enough different and worthwhile ideas to justify the length (or else you will lose marks because of lack of clarity in presentation).

Web browsers are complicated pieces of software. They have evolved into an environment for hosting complete applications that users rely on daily to perform privacy and security critical tasks.

This assignment requires you to write a report comparing privacy protection features that exist in recent versions of Firefox and Chrome. Perform a comparative analysis of the two browsers with respect to the privacy features. Describe the strengths and weaknesses of the features in each browser.

Please consider Mozilla Firefox version 30 or above and Chrome version 35 or above.

You will need to perform independent research for this assignment. To find privacy features you could check the release notes of each browser vendor. Google Scholar is a good way to find published articles, try searching it to see if others have researched the feature you are writing a report on.

Remember to appropriately cite any sources you use in your research.

Marking criteria

Content - 6 marks

To get 3/6 you will need to correctly describe the features, comparing two browsers with respect to the features.

To obtain 5/6 you will need to do the above as well as describe a privacy breach prevented or mitigated by the feature, as well as any possible disadvantages of the feature.

6/6 is available for truly outstanding work.

Organisation and clarity - 4 marks

To gain 2/4 you will need to write clearly and with correct English.

To obtain 3/4 you will need the above and you must organise the material clearly so that the reader finds it easy to get the material they need from your report.

4/4 is available for truly outstanding work.

  Assignment 3 (in class presentation in week 12 or 13)

This assignment requires students to prepare and deliver an oral presentation to the tutorial class. This is a group assignment, done in groups of 4 or 5. Presentation slots will be 15 minutes long and will take place during tutorials in weeks 12 or 13; slots will be allocated in week 11. The group must be available to present in whichever slot they are allocated. The group presentation must be no longer than 10 slides. Before the presentation one member of the group should email a pdf of the slides used to their tutor; also you must hand to the tutor a signed group academic honesty form. The assignment is worth 15 marks.

The assignment requires you to present to the class an account of a recent innovation in security. This could be a new attack, or a new defence, or a new technology. Your presentation should describe the innovation, and discuss some stakeholders, and how they are affected by the innovation. The innovation must have been first reported in 2013 or later.

Marking Criteria

There are 4 marks for the description of the innovation. To gain 2 out of 4, you will need to show a good knowledge of the relevant material. To gain 3 out of 4 you will need to show that you have integrated the knowledge into a coherent understanding of the topic, and of how it differs from the previous state-of-the-art. Full marks is available for truly outstanding work.

There are 4 marks for description of the security impact on stakeholders. To gain 2 out of 4, you will need to show awareness of a range of stakeholders (not missing any important ones), and identify some of their security needs relevant to the innovation. To gain 3 out of 4 you will do the above, and show sophisticated analysis of the way the innovation will impact on the security goals of the stakeholders; as well, you will identify potential conflicts between their needs, or difficulties in meeting their needs. Full marks is available for truly outstanding work.

There are 7 marks for the organization and delivery of your presentation. To gain 4 out of 7, you will need to have every member of the group speak for a reasonable part of the time, and be clearly understood by the listeners. To gain 5 out of 7, you will do the above, and also the presentation will be well-structured so the key points can be clearly identified by listeners, most speakers will project their voice well, and be fluent (not just reading from notes, etc). To gain 6 out of 7 you will need to do all the above, and also you must engage the audience to hold their attention and enhance their interest. Full marks is available for truly outstanding work.
